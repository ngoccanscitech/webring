Our Products

Wagyu omi

Omi beef is a variant of the original Tajima Black Beef cattle, and is known for beautiful marbling, with the sweet fat giving Omi Wagyu Beef a wonderful, mellow taste that sets it apart from other Wagyu.

The oldest established type of Wagyu beef

Omi beef has a history stretching back over 400 years, its history traces back to the Azuchi–Momoyama period, where famed Samurai Takayama Ukon served this tender, marbled beef to warlords.

The perfect surroundings for breeding cattle

Named after Omi province, now known as the Shiga Prefecture where it is exclusively produced, with its rich, natural environment and crystal-clear waters.

Wagyu HIDA

Raised in the Gifu protectorate on the slopes of the Japanese Alps, originally wagyu from the area was known as Gifu beef, with the Hida name emerging in the 1980s.

Hida beef cattle are a variant of the Japanese Black but have their own unique genetics like all types of wagyu. Hida cattle benefit from the clean air and pure water of the region, which gives Hida wagyu beef an incredible flavor.

WOn many awards

Hida beef won the “Wagyu Olympics” in 2002, and awards at the Beef Cattle Expo in 2012, 2013 and 2015.

consistently high quality

Hida beef is easily identifiable thanks to its impressive marbling, and combined with the incredible texture and flavor.
